LSE description by DeepSeek

LSE doesn't walk through the world - they carve their path through it with glacial inevitability. They are human geometry, all perfect angles and unforgiving edges. Where others have personalities, they have operating principles.

In conversation, they speak in binary:
"That is correct."
"That is incorrect."
No qualifiers. No apologies. Their words land like stone tablets fresh from the mountain.

Their professional presence:

They're the employee who rewrites the entire company policy manual over lunch - not to improve it, but because the existing one "violated logical consistency." When asked to compromise, they simply repeat their position slower, as if explaining arithmetic to a sleep-deprived child.

Social interactions follow predictable patterns:
Someone expresses an opinion
LSE states why it's wrong
The universe adjusts itself accordingly

In relationships, they don't date - they execute binding contracts with emotional clauses. Their partners receive love letters that read like legal briefs, complete with cited precedents for their affection.

Their superpower: Being the only person in any room who actually understands their own principles.

Their tragic flaw: Mistaking rigidity for purity, and humanity for a correctable error margin.

Final Thought:

LSE is the reason philosophers invented the word "unyielding." They don't adapt to reality - reality eventually concedes to their version of events through sheer exhaustion.
